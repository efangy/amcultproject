Taiwanese American Student Association: Events

To gain a better understanding of the steps each club took to achieve their “mission”, and since each organization is largely event-based, we looked at the overall scope of the events that they put on. For TASA, we noticed that they put on a variety of different events. Their “core” events are events that are hosted on a yearly basis while other events this year included a few service events, collaboration events with other A/PIA organizations, and a variety of “family / cohort competitions”. Here is a brief overlook into each:

Skate Night

One of TASA’s first events of the year in which members are encouraged to come out after initial mass meeting to get to know one another. Rink is rented out and admission is free for members.

Dumpling Night

Members had the opportunity to make their own dumplings with provided skins and fillings. Board members were available to help out and teach, as well as in charge of cooking the dumplings at each table.

Hotpot Night

Hotpot is a very Taiwanese tradition in which people sit around a boiling pot of water with a variety of lots of different meats and vegetables. Hotpot night took place in three separate locations and people were encouraged to attend the location that their “TASA families” were so that food would be distributed evenly.

Taste of Taiwan

TASA’s biggest event of the year in which they model a venue to look like a night market and offer cultural food and games. More analysis of Taste of Taiwan can be found here.

Mahjong Night

Members come out and learn how to play mahjong, which is a very traditionally played "board game" style game in Taiwan. After a few rounds, members had an opportunity to play mahjong tournament-style for an opportunity to win prizes.

To gain a wider understanding of what the cultural significance behind each event, we talked to both the president of TASA and the current internal programming chair to discover the purpose behind the events they chose:

How do you choose the events that you
It depends on internal programming chair in terms of planning events, but we try our best to replicate certain events every year. But a lot of the things that we have done aren’t necessarily “traditional”, however we’ve been spending a lot of time giving feedback to improve those events.
