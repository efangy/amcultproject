Taiwanese American Student Association: Culture

Analysis:

Many of TASA’s events were very heavily focused on food, as more of their “well-known” events include Hotpot Night, Dumpling Night, and Taste of Taiwan. Even their family systems implemented this year were named after Taiwan’s most famous “dishes” and desserts, such as the “xiao long bao”, “beef noodle soup”, as well as “chua bing”. While it is not entirely possible to reflect an entire culture through the duration of a single year, TASA tries to incorporate as much culture as they can into their events. Most board members that we interviewed also heavily emphasized the importance of “eating” and food customs in Taiwan in general throughout their interviews, which indicates their motivation for continuously implementing food-related events. Food in Taiwanese culture serves to bring people together and also defines social groups. Those who eat together are often indicative of “family” and sharing of food reinforces social connections (“Culture of Taiwan”, 1). Social connections are also a huge aspect of Taiwanese culture, as the basis of social relationships stem from the Taiwanese belief that “one cannot do everything alone and requires cooperation with others” (“Culture of Taiwan”, 1). This indicates the heavy emphasis that Taiwanese culture has on factors such as food as well as family and community, which TASA embodies through their events as well as through the community aspect they introduce within board and with members through the family system. As a result, in terms of cultural aspects, we believe that TASA does succeed in its mission to effectively preserve Taiwanese culture.
