Taiwanese American Student Association: Culture

Taiwanese Cultural Norms in Regards to Food and Events:

The Taiwanese American Student Association at the University of Michigan strives to preserve and promote the understanding of Taiwanese culture on campus. The members of the organization are mostly made up of second-generation Taiwanese American students but is also open to anyone who is interested in learning more about Taiwanese culture in general. The organization boasts a strong mission and vision statements, as shown below, that they strive to accomplish through a variety of different events.

TASA Mission Statement:

Our mission is to develop each member's Taiwanese American identity, explore Taiwanese heritage and culture, reach out to other student organizations as well as the community to establish a supportive and unified network of Taiwanese Americans on campus. We welcome the membership of all persons who shall respect the spirit of TASA.

TASA Vision:

Our vision is to preserve and promote the understanding of Taiwanese culture for second generation Taiwanese American students at U of M and foster networks among fellow Taiwanese Americans (and allies) on campus and the A/PIA, UMich, and greater community.

Quotes from Board Members:

One of the primary questions we asked to gain more insight on the organization was what they personally believed the goals of TASA were:

“I feel like our goal aligns with our mission statement. It's an organization that spreads Taiwanese culture to people on campus but I think as an org in particular it’s a social cultural organization where I think is a nice place to have a community"
- Lilian Sheu, 2018-2019 Co-President

"The main purpose of TASA is to spread Taiwanese community / culture to the general umich community. We’re basically trying to spread Taiwanese community by throwing a variety of different events that show different aspects of Taiwanese culture. For example, we have lots of food nights such as dumpling night, hotpot night, and our largest event of the year, Taste of Taiwan where we try to emulate a night market."
- Curtis Fang, 2018-2019 Co-President

"Honestly, I think the goal of TASA is to spread taiwanese culture and honestly just have a good time"
- Derek Shao, 2018-2019 Freshmen Representative
