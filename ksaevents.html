Korean Student Association: Events

To gain a better understanding of the steps each club took to achieve their “mission”, and since each organization is largely event-based, we looked at the overall scope of the events that they put on. For KSA, we noticed that hosted a few bigger events that seem to be largely "party-based". Here is a brief overlook into each:

  • Events
  • Interviews
  • Analysis

Events:

KSA Welcome Party

KSA hosts a party during the first month of school to welcome everyone back into the KSA community at the University of Michigan, with a special emphasis on welcoming freshman through a KSA hosted freshman pregame. Like most of their parties, it was throw in the Red Room at Necto Nightclub.



KSA Family Reveal 2.0

This was the main event for the concept of families in KSA. According to the membership chair for the 2018-2019 school year, families were created with the idea of “More intimate way [for members to hangout]; another “friend group” and you would hangout with them and you would do different activities.” This event revealed the different families within KSA and allowed for them to meet each other.


KSA x KISA: Blackout

Annual Halloween party hosted by both KSA and KISA (Korean International Student Association).





KSA Culture Show

The culture show is the largest even KSA puts on through the year. This event showcases various student organizations and performance groups that aim to promote Korean culture in traditional and modern forms.

Subtle Asian Occasion

An event aimed towards freshman meeting each other in a larger APIA setting. It consisted of a “huge mix of activities at one time, we had bubble tea, we had smash going on, we had people taking pictures on the backdrop (Will Park, Internal VP).


KSA x LiPHE - Ballin For Bones

Ballin’ For Bones is a basketball tournament organized by KSA and LPhiE where 8 separate teams (organizations) will compete for prizes. There will also be a bone marrow cheek swab table where players and spectators can volunteer to get their cheek swabbed for a philanthropic cause towards a bone marrow drive. All the proceeds will go toward funding for rented basketball courts and prizes.

Interviews

We did not get the chance to interview KSA's Event Chair, but we did gain a number of different responses regarding KSA events through KSA's Treasurer, VP Internal, President, as well as their Freshman Representative.
On the KSA Culture Show…
“KSA is basically trying to spread Korean culture and awareness on campus and Korean based events and showcasing what Korean culture is about and that’s basically the premise of the Korean Show, showcasing Kpop singing/dancing, taekwondo, drama and related to korean dramas”
-Michael Kang, 2018-2019 Treasurer
On parties…
“Yeah, any event with Korean Americans will learn, [there are] countless amount of people who are from South Korea, you learn about what they’re like, their perspectives, etc”
- Will Park, 2018-2019 VP Internal

“It’s okay just because we’re in college, and we think that way can help create that community”
- Ben Kim, President
On Subtle Asian Occasion…
“Subtle Asian Occasion was an event focused on allowing individuals in the Asian American (or for KSA’s Korean American and those interested in Asian culture) to socialize and mingle, all while bonding through similar and relatable characteristics across Asian cultures. This event capitalized on the trending “Subtle Asian Traits” and “Subtle Asian Dating” Facebook group phenomena”
- Justin Minn, 2018-2019 Freshman Representative
On Ballin for Bones…
“Ballin for Bones was an opportunity for KSA to collaborate with Asian-interest Fraternity Lambda Phi Epsilon in their philanthropic dedication with the National Marrow Donor Program. This basketball tournament was a way for on campus organizations to “ball for a purpose”. The event consisted of a basketball tournament and a National Marrow Donor Program information and cheek swabbing booth where players, organization members, and spectators can volunteer to get their cheeks swabbed for and towards a philanthropic cause”
- Justin Minn, 2018-2019 Freshman Representative

Analysis

Culture is clearly emphasized and distinguished in the biggest event of the year, the KSA Culture Show. This event highlights both the traditional and modern cultures of Korea through performances ranging from Taekwondo and a traditional drumming set to K-Pop dancing and singing. It also works to bring in a Korean-American nature through the incorporation of popular American songs in Seoul Juice (singing group) and DB3’s (dancing) performances. However, most of the other events hosted by KSA are not as culturally significant. Every year, KSA hosts 3-4 parties at Necto Nightclub during point times, such as the Welcome Party, a Halloween Party, and a KSA Culture Show afterparty. These events don’t necessarily align with any Korean holidays or events, but rather reflect American holidays or “college culture.” The events that KSA hosted this year were clearly more “large-scale, entertainment events,” designed to bring in as many people into the events as possible and delivering an entertaining and memorable time for everyone.
