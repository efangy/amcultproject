KSA x TASA

Presence + Impact on Campus - AMCULT 352 Term Project by Elena Fang and Inwoo Cho

Overview & Objective:

Our overall objective is to analyze the impact of Korean Student Association and Taiwanese American Student Association on the University of Michigan campus. Ethnicity is a huge part of what people tie to their identity and being able to spread culture has largely been an objective of both clubs of KSA and TASA. Throughout this website, we'll be analyzing several aspects of both organizations on campus to see how well they reflect each respective culture and how well they have accomplished their prospective missions. Scroll down to get a bigger overview of our objectives and navigate through the links on the left to read our analysis.

  • Learn More

Key Points

Culture

We will be looking at the culture of each organization and compare them to the cultural history and background of Taiwan and Korea.

Community

We will be taking a look at both the club dyanmic, board dynamic, as well as cultural norms of community in Taiwan and Korea.

Events

We will be looking at the nature of the events that KSA and TASA put on and analyze them to see how well they carry out their mission and goals.

Changes

Based on how each organization operates as a whole, we will be suggesting changes as to how to better achieve their missions.
