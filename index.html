KSA x TASA

Presence + Impact on Campus - AMCULT 352 Term Project by Elena Fang and Inwoo Cho

Overview & Objective:

Our overall objective is to analyze the impact of Korean Student Association and Taiwanese American Student Association on the University of Michigan campus. Throughout this website, we'll be analyzing several aspects of both organizations on campus to see how well they reflect each respective culture and how well they have accomplished their prospective missions. In our research, we hypothesized that TASA seems to host more “small-scale, interactive events” while KSA seems to host more “large-scale, entertaining events”. We believe by hosting these types of events, we can attribute these events to the culture of Taiwan as well as Korea. Because these are two groups on the University of Michigan campus in which we are personally involved with, we hope to see how well TASA and KSA may have played a role in solidifying the identities of those who have been a part of them. Scroll down to get a bigger overview of our objectives and navigate through the links on the left to read our analysis.

Key Points

Culture

We will be looking at the culture of each organization and compare them to the cultural history and background of Taiwan and Korea.

Community

We will be taking a look at the club dynamic, board dynamic, as well as cultural norms of community in Taiwan and Korea.

Events

We will be looking at the nature of the events that KSA and TASA put on and analyze them to see how successfully they carry out their mission.

Changes

Based on how each organization operates as a whole, we will be suggesting changes as to how to better achieve their goals.
