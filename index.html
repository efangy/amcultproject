KSA x TASA

Presence + Impact on Campus - AMCULT 352 Term Project by Elena Fang and Inwoo Cho

Overview & Objective:

Our overall objective is to analyze the impact of Korean Student Association and Taiwanese American Student Association on the University of Michigan campus. Throughout this website, we'll be analyzing several aspects of both organizations on campus to see how well they reflect each respective culture and how well they have accomplished their prospective missions. In our research, we hypothesized that TASA seems to host more “small-scale, interactive events” while KSA seems to host more “large-scale, entertaining events”. We believe by hosting these types of events, we can attribute these events to the culture of Taiwan as well as Korea. Because these are two groups on the University of Michigan campus in which we are personally involved with, we hope to see how well TASA and KSA may have played a role in solidifying the identities of those who have been a part of them. Key Points

Overview

We will be looking at the overall mission statements and vision of the organizations.

Culture

We will be looking at the culture of each organization and compare them to the cultural history and background of Taiwan and Korea.

Events

We will be looking at the nature of the events that KSA and TASA put on and analyze them to see how successfully they carry out their mission.

Community

We will be taking a look at the club dynamic, board dynamic, as well as cultural norms of community in Taiwan and Korea.

Data Collected:

In order to learn more about the purpose of each organization and get more insight into the background and cultural significance of their events, we interviewed several board members of both the Korean Student Association and the Taiwanese American Student Association. This included:

TASA

Lilian Sheu: 2018-2019 Co-President
Curtis Fang: 2018-2019 Co President
Serena Yee: 2018-2019 Internal Programming Chair, 2019-2020 Co-President
Max Tsao: 2018-2019 Internal Programming Chair
Brian Lin: 2018-2019 Family Relations Chair
Derek Shao: 2018-2019 Freshman Representative

KSA

Ben Kim: 2018-2019 President
Michael Kang: 2019-2020 President
William Park: 2018-2019 VP Internal
Yujin Kim: 2018-2019 Membership Chair
Justin Minn: 2018-2019 Freshman Representative
Most of our questions were tailored to the position that each board member served, but general questions included:

  • What is the main purpose of your organization (KSA/TASA) as a whole?
  • What is the main purpose of your culture show or the event (Taste of Taiwan)? Was it more for entertainment value or for cultural significance?
  • What was the decision making process behind what you chose to have in your event? (i.e. focus on food, games, performances) Do you believe this impacted the way people perceived your culture?
  • Do you have any personal experiences that speak to your culture and its representation on campus?
  • How do you perceive your orgs presence on campus/in APIA community right now? How do you think other people view it?
  • What’s the board dynamic like?
  • What are some changes you want to implement next year in terms of events? What do you think are good ways to bring the community closer together?
